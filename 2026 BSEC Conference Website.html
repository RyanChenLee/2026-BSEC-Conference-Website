About the Conference

A milestone two decades in the making

In 2006, a vision was born at Babson College: to create a community where entrepreneurs could tackle the world's most pressing sustainability and energy challenges. Two decades later, Entrepreneurs for Impact brings that community back together.

Join students, alumni, founders, investors, and ecosystem partners for a day of bold ideas, high-impact networking, and honest conversations about building ventures that matter.

This isn't just another conference. It's a celebration of 20 years of student leadership, innovation, and the entrepreneurs who've turned sustainability challenges into scalable solutions — and a reunion of the founders, alumni, and partners who built this community from the ground up.

From the wind turbine in 2008, to EV charging stations via a Toyota partnership in 2012, to a solar farm, bee farm, and Miyawaki forest all launching in the same year as the 20th anniversary conference — this is what happens when entrepreneurs commit to impact.

Keynote: David Evans Shaw

A lifelong builder of companies, ideas, and impact. Pioneer behind more than a dozen science-driven enterprises valued at over $50 billion. David’s work spans business, public health, ocean conservation, and beyond — united by a single idea: leadership success should be measured by impact.

David Evans Shaw
CEO, Black Point Group
Author, Wave Making: Inspired By Impact

David Evans Shaw is a lifelong business and social entrepreneur—a builder of companies, ideas, and impact. As CEO of Black Point Group, he has helped create more than a dozen science-driven enterprises valued at over $50 billion, advancing healthcare and innovation worldwide.

But his work extends far beyond business—into education, science policy, public health, international affairs, the arts, capitalism reform, mental health, and the global conservation of nature including oceans.

He has championed the powerful idea that leadership success should be measured by impact. In his book Wave Making: Inspired By Impact, he invites all of us to step forward—not as spectators, but as changemakers… or as he calls them: wave makers… for a better world.

Beyond business, David has been a leading voice in global ocean stewardship — championing science-based conservation, marine policy, and sustainable ocean economies as a pathway to a healthier planet.

At this year’s 20th Annual BSEC Conference, David will receive the inaugural Entrepreneur for Impact Award in recognition of his decades of leadership building enterprises that measure success by their impact on people and the planet.
